# Email Templates

## 1. Cold Outreach Email

### Version A: Pain-Focused

**Subject:** Quick question about [specific process]

> Hi [Name],
>
> I noticed [Company Name] is growing quickly — congrats!
>
> Quick question: How much time does your team spend on [specific manual task, e.g., "entering lead data into your CRM" or "chasing unpaid invoices"]?
>
> I help [industry] companies automate repetitive work so they can focus on growth. Most of my clients save 10+ hours/week.
>
> Worth a quick chat?
>
> [Your Name]
>
> P.S. Here's a quick win I built for a similar company: [one-sentence case study]

### Version B: Curiosity-Focused

**Subject:** Saw something about [Company Name]

> Hi [Name],
>
> I was looking at [Company Name] and noticed you're using [Tool 1] and [Tool 2].
>
> Curious — are those connected? I ask because most teams I work with have data stuck in silos, leading to manual work and missed opportunities.
>
> I specialize in connecting these tools so information flows automatically. Takes about a week to set up, saves 10+ hours/week.
>
> Interested in a quick call to see if there's a fit?
>
> [Your Name]

### Version C: Value-First

**Subject:** Free automation audit for [Company Name]

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm offering a free 15-minute automation audit for [industry] businesses this month.
>
> Here's what you'll get:
>
> - 3 specific automation opportunities for your business
> - Estimated time savings (usually 10-20 hours/week)
> - Quick implementation roadmap
>
> No pitch, no obligation. Just useful insights.
>
> Want me to book you in?
>
> [Your Name]

## 2. Testimonial Request Email

**Subject:** Quick favor? (2 minutes)

> Hi [Name],
>
> It's been [X weeks] since we finished your automations. How's everything running?
>
> Quick favor: Would you mind sharing a few sentences about your experience? It really helps other businesses trust the process.
>
> Here are some prompts if helpful:
>
> - What was your biggest challenge before we worked together?
> - How much time are you saving now?
> - Would you recommend this to others?
>
> A few sentences is perfect. I'll share a draft for your approval before posting anywhere.
>
> Thanks so much!
>
> [Your Name]
